The Anatomy of Link Building Explained: What You Should Know?

Link building is a pretty powerful technique that can boost your rankings wildly if you get to understand it well enough. It can also be your worst nightmare and the cause of your site’s complete downfall if not worked right.



Both ways, it is an amazing SEO technique that has been working for many people for decades now. In fact, up until now it is has been a significant contribution to wonderful rankings of many authoritative sites you know out there in many niches.



The biggest problem though with many people who use link building in their sites is that they regularly make use of bad practices that are set on cutting corners on responsibilities to achieve easy rankings. Google is comfortably able to capture and punish those that use these bad strategies using frequently algorithm updates.


These tricks commonly known as black hat linking. You need to avoid these techniques in every possible way if you want to make your website become an authority in your niche and achieve the level of success you have in mind.


The best way for you to keep off these bad practices is through getting to understand the very anatomy of link building. Having known the most important aspects of link building, you will be able to find out what’s best and what’s not and how to make the most of what’s best.


So to help you get the gist of this useful SEO technique here is what you need to know about link building:



  1. What is Link Building?




This is basically having external links that point to your website on other external pages and posts of other websites. Link building gives more exposure to your site, get better rankings on popular search engines and direct a good deal of traffic to your site for conversions or sales or any other intended purpose.


If you have links, especially in websites that have ranked in the first three positions in Google, Bing or Yahoo, it may have a huge positive impact regarding exposure on your site. Just take a second and think, how much traffic does the website that’s ranked the first get every day?


A huge load of it! And what if you could tap into it and share the traffic with your site? That would really make a huge difference in your site.


So if you can manage to talk that site’s owner into letting you leave a link or a couple of them there, you can get to see a significant rise in rankings in your online business.


And that’s how link building came to be!




  1. No, Follow vs. Do Follow Links




The do follow types of links have been around for a long time, but a no follow link has been around since 2005, after a dire need to solve the issue of spammers of other people’s sites securing good places for their sites in the search engines. Let’s catch a good glimpse of these types of links.



  • A do follow link is a link that is counted as a point by Google search bots and adds more into the link juice that boosts a site’s rankings in the SERPs. What this basically means is that it is a link left on a website (like New York Times or Forbes), and it passes link power to the site where the link is pointing.


Having more of these shows Google that many people are linking to a certain page to your site, which then means that the page must be really good. And in Google’s effort to deliver quality to their users, they will rank the page higher in the SERP’s to help many people find it and enjoy it as well.



  • A no follow link is a link that doesn’t count in the placing of your site in the search engines. There is no link juice for this kind of link, which means that it’s not going to make any difference to the site of the person who left it.


There is actually a HTML attribute for this link. Here it is -> ” <a href=”http://www.website.com/”; rel=”nofollow”>Link Text</a>”


What the attribute says is that this link should not count on its players. As said earlier, this link came to be after many people realized that the most inbound links there were for their sites, no matter where or how could gain them a boost.


This later leads to a messy situation where links were all over the place, and now it was not a matter of authenticity but a dirty competition to win good positions. So getting the links not to make any difference or bring any pointers to their destination was the perfect solution.


Are no follow links worth it then?


Sure they are! Although they don’t exert any SEO energy, they usually serve a good way of getting referral traffic that can then be channel the traffic your site and give you a lot of leads and conversions too! If well placed as a blog comment or a post it can really make more impact than rankings would.


The main reason for fighting for good rankings is to get more traffic, and if you get leads and conversions out of the no follow links instead of rankings, then they are well worth the short!




  1. Importance of Link Building in Ranking a Keyword




If you are looking for making money online then you must rank your money keywords on Google top three positions. Link building is the way to go! If you have been wondering how to rank a keyword that you have found a fit for awesome conversions and would want to have a good placing under it, inbound links can help you do that.


What you will only need to do is to write a good article on that keyword, a good informative and accurate content on that specific keyword and then get quality links for it once you have published it.


What importance will this serve?


Well, link building is all about leaving traces and tracks of your content in other people’s websites and social media. If you can manage leave as many traces as possible, it will make search engines aware of the great influence and link your article gets and how many clicks the article is getting too.


They will perceive this way; if many people love this article, it must be a great one so let’s rank it on top so that all people can benefit from it also, as explained earlier. That’s how you are going to get a better ranking for that keyword.

There are many legit ways that search engines accept for bloggers to build links using, but there are those ways that give outstanding results.   • Guest Posting – writing articles for an influential blog is one of the ways you can use to link your site to that blog. If the blog is good enough, you should be seeing results in days. What happens is that search engines rank influential sites quick, which means the article you will have written as a guest post will go on the web real quick.

Things to Avoid When Building Links


There are some things you are going to want to keep away as do your link building if you want this tactic to add value to your website or blog. What will be listed below will only harm your campaigns and reputation in search engines and loose trust as well as good positions in the SERPs? So here is what you should avoid at all costs:



  • PBNs – As discussed earlier, private blog.comworks are a black hat method of achieving rankings. This is trying to creating a place for your site to thrive using your own blogs and leading engines to believe you are getting links from outside when in fact you are not. Many sites have been penalized heavily for this to the point of getting banned. Others have been ranked in the last pages and have never recovered from that for years.

  • Avoid over-optimized anchor texts – Anchor text plays a significant role in ranking your site. Moreover, if your link profile constitutes a lot of exact matches and commercial anchor text, then it attracts penalty from Google. You should diversify your anchor text and focus more on brand, generic and naked URLs.


To summarize all that you should avoid, only one thing can be said, don’t try to fool Google or any other engine. It’s not worth it. Just try to build upon your branding. Make sure you are building quality links the right and accepted way, and this will earn you points and most importantly, trust, which is even stronger than points.
